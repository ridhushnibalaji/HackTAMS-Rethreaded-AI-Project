SUSTAINABILITY

Overconsumption

Fast fashion encourages overconsumption by constantly releasing new trends at extremely low prices. Brands drop new collections weekly, making shoppers feel pressure to keep up with ever-changing styles. Because the clothing is inexpensive, many items are worn only a few times before being discarded. This leads to massive amounts of textile waste in landfills and people buying more than necessary.

Pollution

Growing cotton requires enormous amounts of water, while fabric dyeing releases toxic chemicals into rivers and soil. Many garments are made from synthetic materials like polyester, which release microplastics into oceans when washed. In addition, the global shipping of millions of garments increases carbon emissions. The fashion industry is one of the major contributors to water pollution, waste, and climate change.

Poor Working Conditions

Fast fashion relies on cheap labor, often in countries without proper protections, which leads to unsafe and unfair working environments. Employees are paid extremely low wages, required to work long hours, and placed in overcrowded factories. These conditions persist because companies prioritize low cost and fast production over worker safety and fair treatment, allowing exploitation to continue..
